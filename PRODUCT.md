# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Casual daily checker — regular person on phone or desktop, opening the app during a quick morning routine before heading out. They want one confident answer fast and don't need to understand the meteorology behind it. The humor is a pleasant surprise, not the reason they opened the app.

## Product Purpose

Umbrella Maybe answers one question: should you bring an umbrella today? It strips weather data down to a single binary-ish verdict (yes / maybe / no) with a witty byline that makes the answer memorable. Success looks like a user glancing at the screen, getting the answer, and closing the tab with a small smile.

## Brand Personality

Dry. Confident. Quietly funny.

The interface plays it completely straight — premium, dark, precise. The personality lives entirely in the copy (bylines, humor modes). The UI never winks at you; the words do all the winking.

## Anti-references

- Generic weather apps (AccuWeather, Weather.com) — blue sky gradients, corporate information density, forgettable.
- SaaS dashboards — metrics cards, gradient heroes, "built for teams" energy. This is a personal tool.
- Childish or cartoon — bubbly fonts, over-cute icons, bright primary colors. The humor is dry, not silly.
- Over-designed / noisy — too many colors, excessive animation, trying too hard to be memorable. The craft is quiet.

## Design Principles

1. **Straight face, sharp tongue.** The visual design is premium and restrained; the comedy lives only in the words. No visual exclamation marks, no playful shapes.
2. **One question, one answer.** Every layout decision serves the single-question format. Anything that competes with the yes/maybe/no verdict gets cut or demoted.
3. **Confident at a glance.** The umbrella status must be readable within one second, before the eye even reaches the supporting copy.
4. **Premium restraint.** Dark, polished, nothing decorative. Apple Weather clarity meets Vercel dark precision. Every element earns its place.
5. **Personality through precision.** The craft of the UI (spacing, type weight, animation timing, icon quality) should feel as considered as the bylines themselves.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Dark mode is the primary theme. All body text must hit 4.5:1 contrast against background. Reduced motion must be respected — animations degrade to instant crossfades. No color-only information conveyance (umbrella status uses text and icon, not color alone).
